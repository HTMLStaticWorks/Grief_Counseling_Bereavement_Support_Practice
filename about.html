Our Story

A space built on empathy, designed to hold the weight of your sorrow.

Dr. Sarah Jenkins

Founder & Clinical Psychologist

I began NewLeaf out of a deep belief that grief is not an illness to be cured, but a profound human experience that requires a compassionate witness. Over my 15 years in practice, I have had the privilege of walking alongside hundreds of individuals as they navigate the darkest chapters of their lives.

Credentials & Accreditations

  • Ph.D. in Clinical Psychology
  • Certified Grief Counseling Specialist (CGCS)
  • Trained in EMDR for Trauma Processing

Our Philosophy of Care

A trauma-informed approach centered on your emotional safety.

Radical Empathy

We listen without judgment or an agenda to "fix" you. Your feelings, however complex, are valid and welcome here.

Trauma-Informed

We recognize the profound impact that loss has on the nervous system. Our pace is slow, careful, and deeply respectful of your emotional limits.

Honoring Continuity

We believe in finding ways to carry the memory of your loved one forward, integrating the loss into a meaningful new chapter of life.

Our Journey

Milestones in building a sanctuary for healing.

2010 — The Foundation

Dr. Jenkins begins her specialized focus in grief and bereavement while working in a dedicated hospice care setting, witnessing the profound need for continued aftercare.

2016 — NewLeaf is Born

NewLeaf Grief Counseling opens its doors as a solo practice, providing a quiet, dedicated space away from clinical hospital environments for true, unhurried healing.

2021 — Expanding Support

The practice grows to include specialized group therapy sessions and introduces a secure telehealth platform to support clients navigating grief during a time of global isolation.

Today — A Community of Healing

NewLeaf continues to serve as a beacon of hope, expanding our trauma-informed resources and remaining deeply committed to honoring every unique journey through loss.
